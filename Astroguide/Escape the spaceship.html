Escape the Spaceship

Escape the Spaceship is a multilevel adventure game based upon the butterfly effect, with small changes effecting decisions and characters you may meet later in the game. The preface is simple, Escape the Spaceship. Ever time you run into a problem, you will be given a set of items with which you may attempt to solve the problem. Make no mistake, though, the game may feel it important to kill you and subsequentially mock you mercilessly if you make any sort of stupid mistake. For example, using an explosive to diffuse a bomb will most likely result in immediate and painful death.

This brings us to the next great part of this game. The game will restart you from this screen whenever you die. That way, you can go through the entire game again making the right choices. Be warned, though, there is more than one way through this game, and even more ways to die horrible deaths. There are currently over one hundred different options! But don't think you have to die alone! This game inputs the "body count" system. Throughout the game, your decisions will most likely cause horrible deaths for many humans and aliens alike. But don't think you can just forget about all the deaths you've caused. The game will keep track. Every time you die, the game will happily inform you how much of a terrible person you were.

However, when you click start, remember this. There is no going back, unless you die. This means that if you feel you have made the wrong choice, your sunk. It was your fault, and it will probably come back to bite you in the future. Anyhow, the whole point of this paragraph is to take up space on the opening page. So, was our tutorial long enough?
